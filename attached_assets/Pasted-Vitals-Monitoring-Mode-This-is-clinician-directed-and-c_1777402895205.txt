Vitals Monitoring Mode:
This is clinician-directed and can be turned on from the patient profile by staff/provider.

This is different from optional wellness tracking.

Vitals Monitoring Mode examples:
- 7-day blood pressure log
- 10-day blood pressure + heart rate log
- 14-day weight monitoring
- Custom monitoring period

Clinician can configure:
- Vital types to monitor:
  - Blood pressure
  - Heart rate
  - Weight
- Duration:
  - 7 days
  - 10 days
  - 14 days
  - custom
- Frequency:
  - daily
  - twice daily
  - custom
- Start date
- End date
- Optional instructions

Patient side:
If Vitals Monitoring Mode is active, show a clear card:
“Your provider is monitoring your blood pressure for 10 days.”

Only show the requested vital fields.

For BP:
- systolic
- diastolic
- time of reading
- heart rate if selected
- notes
- symptoms checkbox:
  - headache
  - chest pain
  - shortness of breath
  - dizziness
  - vision changes
  - weakness
  - confusion
  - none

If patient has not logged the requested vital for that day, show reminder:
“Hey! Your provider is waiting for your daily blood pressure reading. Don’t forget to log it today.”

When the monitoring period ends:
- Automatically mark monitoring episode completed.
- Stop patient reminders for that episode.
- Stop active vitals prompt.
- Send provider inbox notification:
“[Patient Name] has completed their 10-day blood pressure log. View results.”

Clicking notification should open the patient profile and open the contained Monitoring/Daily Check-In tool inside that profile.

Provider alerts:
Create provider inbox notifications for concerning patterns.

For blood pressure:
Stage 2 definition:
systolic >= 140 OR diastolic >= 90

If patient logs more than 2 Stage 2 readings during the monitoring period, notify provider:
“[Patient Name] has logged multiple Stage 2 blood pressure readings. Review vitals log.”

Severe BP:
systolic >= 180 OR diastolic >= 120

Provider notification:
“Urgent BP reading logged for [Patient Name]: [reading]. Review immediately.”

Patient-facing message for severe BP:
“This reading is very high. Please recheck after sitting quietly for 5 minutes. If it remains this high or you have chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache, weakness, confusion, or vision changes, seek urgent/emergency care.”

Do not tell patient to change medication.
Do not diagnose.
Do not replace provider judgment.

Provider/staff UX:
From provider/staff standpoint, this must also remain clean.

Add a contained tool inside the patient profile called:
“Monitoring & Check-Ins”

This should open inside the patient profile, not as a separate page and not mixed throughout the chart.

Provider/staff should be able to view:
- Current patient-selected mode: Off / Standard / Power
- Active clinician-directed vitals monitoring episodes
- Recent daily logs
- Weight trend
- Food consistency summary
- Sleep trend
- Exercise trend
- Mood/symptom trend
- Medication/supplement adherence
- Patient-added medications/supplements
- Cycle data if applicable
- Vitals trends
- AI-generated summaries
- Flags

Provider inbox:
Simple notifications should alert providers/staff when needed.

Notification types:
- vitals_monitoring_completed
- stage_2_bp_pattern
- severe_bp_reading
- missed_required_vital_log
- unexpected_bleeding_reported
- patient_added_med_or_supplement
- worsening_symptom_trend

Missed vitals:
If clinician-required vitals are active and patient misses 2 days, notify provider:
“[Patient Name] has missed 2 required blood pressure logs.”

Vitals integration:
Any vitals logged by the patient should integrate with vitals taken in-clinic for trending purposes.

However, patient-logged vitals must be clearly labeled and color-coded as patient-documented.

In vitals trends:
- In-clinic vitals should remain distinct.
- Patient-documented vitals should be color-coded differently.
- Tooltip/label should say “Patient-documented”
- In-clinic vitals should say “Clinic-documented”

Do not overwrite clinic vitals.
Do not mix the source invisibly.
Both should trend together visually, but source should be clear.
