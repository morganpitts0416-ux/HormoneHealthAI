We need to build a new optional, modular patient-facing and provider-facing tool inside ClinIQ called “Daily Check-In” for now.

This feature should be built as a contained tool, not something scattered throughout the app.

Primary goal:
Create an optional patient tracking/accountability tool that helps patients log health behaviors, vitals, medication/supplement adherence, symptoms, weight, sleep, food, exercise, and cycle-related information when appropriate. The tool should generate helpful insights for patients and provide clean, clinically useful summaries for staff/providers.

This must NOT clutter the app.
This must NOT be required for every patient.
This must NOT disrupt the existing patient portal, lab workflow, encounter workflow, provider dashboard, or chart structure.

Core concept:
There are patient-selected modes and clinician-directed monitoring.

Patient-selected modes:
1. Off
2. Standard Mode
3. Power Mode

Clinician-directed mode:
4. Vitals Monitoring Mode

Vitals Monitoring Mode can run at the same time as Standard or Power Mode.

Patient Portal UX:
Add a clean card on the patient dashboard called “Daily Check-In” or “Accountability Buddy.”

If the patient has not opted in, show a simple opt-in screen:
“Want a little accountability buddy?”
“Track your weight, food, sleep, exercise, symptoms, medications, and progress so your care team can better understand your health.”

Buttons:
- Turn On
- No Thanks

Patients should also be able to turn this tool OFF later from patient settings.
Patients should be able to opt back IN later from patient settings.

If turned OFF:
- Do not show tracking reminders.
- Do not show daily check-in prompts.
- Keep the portal clean.

If turned ON:
- Allow patient to choose Standard Mode or Power Mode.
- Patient can switch between Standard and Power Mode later in settings.
- Patient can turn the feature off later.

Navigation requirement:
When the patient is inside the Daily Check-In tool, they should always have a clear way to return to:
- Main Dashboard
- Other tools/sections inside the app

User UI is extremely important. This tool should feel easy, clean, and contained — not like the patient is lost in a separate app.

Patient tool layout:
Use a clean two-column layout.

Left side:
A vertical icon rail that stays visible.

Icons/categories:
- Today
- Weight
- Food
- Sleep
- Exercise
- Medications/Supplements
- Mood/Symptoms
- Cycle, only for female patients
- Vitals, only if active or enabled
- Insights/Reports

Main content area:
The selected section opens in the main content area.
The left icon rail remains visible.
Do not make this feel like separate pages.

Add a “Back to Dashboard” button at the top of the tool.

Standard Mode:
Simple daily tracking:
- Weight
- Food
- Sleep
- Exercise
- Mood/symptoms
- Medication/supplement adherence
- Menstrual/cycle tracking only for female patients when appropriate

Food tracking in Standard Mode:
Do NOT build MyFitnessPal in Standard Mode.

Fields:
- Protein: low / moderate / strong
- Water: low / moderate / strong
- Fiber/veggies: low / moderate / strong
- Processed/sugary foods: none / some / high
- Alcohol: yes/no
- Optional notes

Add patient guidance ranges:
Protein:
- Low: less than 60g/day
- Moderate: 60–90g/day
- Strong: 90g+/day

Water:
- Low: less than 40 oz/day
- Moderate: 40–80 oz/day
- Strong: 80+ oz/day

Fiber/veggies:
- Low: 0–1 servings
- Moderate: 2–3 servings
- Strong: 4+ servings

Allow clinic admin/provider to customize these ranges later.

Power Mode:
Patient-selected optional advanced tracking.

Includes everything in Standard Mode, plus more detailed fields:
- Calories optional
- Protein grams
- Carbs optional
- Fat optional
- Fiber grams optional
- Water ounces
- Meal notes
- More detailed exercise logging
- More detailed AI insights

Power Mode should feel like:
“I want more accountability and more detailed feedback.”

AI insights in Power Mode should be more detailed and data-driven, but still warm and non-shaming.

Daily reminder bubble:
If patient tracking mode is Standard or Power and there has been no activity for 3 days, show a friendly dismissible talk-bubble on the patient dashboard.

Example:
“Hey friend — it’s been a few days since your last check-in. A quick update helps your care team see the full picture.”

Actions:
- Check in now
- Dismiss

If dismissed, hide it for that session/day.

Medications/Supplements section:
Pull active medications and supplements from the patient’s profile.

For each medication/supplement:
- Show name
- Dose
- Frequency if available
- Checkbox for “taken today”
- For weekly injections, allow “taken this week”
- Allow patient to backfill missed days
- Include “I skipped this” with optional reason

Important:
Patients should also be able to add medications or supplements that are not already listed.

When patient adds a medication or supplement:
- Add it to the patient’s medication/supplement list in their profile.
- Mark it as patient-reported.
- Show it as patient-added/patient-reported for staff/providers.
- Include it in future analysis and insights.
- Starting the next day, it should appear as a checkbox item in the medication/supplement adherence section.

This matters because patients often take extra medications/supplements without telling the clinic. The system should help identify these because they may be helping, interfering, duplicative, unsafe, or clinically relevant.

Medication/supplement AI logic:
The insight engine should evaluate patient-reported meds/supplements and flag possible concerns for provider review, such as:
- duplicate supplements
- potentially excessive doses
- possible interactions
- supplements that may affect BP, sleep, GI symptoms, bleeding, hormones, or labs
- patient taking something that may explain symptoms or lab changes

Do not alarm the patient.
Do not tell the patient to stop prescription medication.
Use patient-facing language like:
“You added a new supplement. Your care team may want to review this to make sure it fits safely with your plan.”

Mood/Symptoms section:
Track:
- Mood 1–5
- Energy 1–5
- Cravings 1–5
- Hunger 1–5
- Brain fog 1–5
- Anxiety/irritability 1–5
- GI symptoms: nausea, constipation, diarrhea, reflux, bloating
- Other symptoms text box

Cycle section:
Only show cycle tracking for female patients.

When the patient opts in to the tool, include setup questions:
- Do you currently have menstrual cycles? yes/no
- Are your cycles regular? yes/no/not sure
- Are you using hormone therapy? yes/no/not sure
- Have you had a hysterectomy? yes/no
- Do you still have ovaries? yes/no/not sure

If patient says they do not have cycles, do not show full cycle tracking by default.

However, even if patient says they do not have cycles, there must still be a way to document:
- breakthrough bleeding
- spotting
- unexpected bleeding
- postmenopausal bleeding

This can live inside Mood/Symptoms as a symptom option:
“Unexpected bleeding / spotting”

If selected, flag as clinically relevant for staff/provider review.

Vitals Monitoring Mode:
This is clinician-directed and can be turned on from the patient profile by staff/provider.

This is different from optional wellness tracking.

Vitals Monitoring Mode examples:
- 7-day blood pressure log
- 10-day blood pressure + heart rate log
- 14-day weight monitoring
- Custom monitoring period

Clinician can configure:
- Vital types to monitor:
  - Blood pressure
  - Heart rate
  - Weight
- Duration:
  - 7 days
  - 10 days
  - 14 days
  - custom
- Frequency:
  - daily
  - twice daily
  - custom
- Start date
- End date
- Optional instructions

Patient side:
If Vitals Monitoring Mode is active, show a clear card:
“Your provider is monitoring your blood pressure for 10 days.”

Only show the requested vital fields.

For BP:
- systolic
- diastolic
- time of reading
- heart rate if selected
- notes
- symptoms checkbox:
  - headache
  - chest pain
  - shortness of breath
  - dizziness
  - vision changes
  - weakness
  - confusion
  - none

If patient has not logged the requested vital for that day, show reminder:
“Hey! Your provider is waiting for your daily blood pressure reading. Don’t forget to log it today.”

When the monitoring period ends:
- Automatically mark monitoring episode completed.
- Stop patient reminders for that episode.
- Stop active vitals prompt.
- Send provider inbox notification:
“[Patient Name] has completed their 10-day blood pressure log. View results.”

Clicking notification should open the patient profile and open the contained Monitoring/Daily Check-In tool inside that profile.

Provider alerts:
Create provider inbox notifications for concerning patterns.

For blood pressure:
Stage 2 definition:
systolic >= 140 OR diastolic >= 90

If patient logs more than 2 Stage 2 readings during the monitoring period, notify provider:
“[Patient Name] has logged multiple Stage 2 blood pressure readings. Review vitals log.”

Severe BP:
systolic >= 180 OR diastolic >= 120

Provider notification:
“Urgent BP reading logged for [Patient Name]: [reading]. Review immediately.”

Patient-facing message for severe BP:
“This reading is very high. Please recheck after sitting quietly for 5 minutes. If it remains this high or you have chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache, weakness, confusion, or vision changes, seek urgent/emergency care.”

Do not tell patient to change medication.
Do not diagnose.
Do not replace provider judgment.

Provider/staff UX:
From provider/staff standpoint, this must also remain clean.

Add a contained tool inside the patient profile called:
“Monitoring & Check-Ins”

This should open inside the patient profile, not as a separate page and not mixed throughout the chart.

Provider/staff should be able to view:
- Current patient-selected mode: Off / Standard / Power
- Active clinician-directed vitals monitoring episodes
- Recent daily logs
- Weight trend
- Food consistency summary
- Sleep trend
- Exercise trend
- Mood/symptom trend
- Medication/supplement adherence
- Patient-added medications/supplements
- Cycle data if applicable
- Vitals trends
- AI-generated summaries
- Flags

Provider inbox:
Simple notifications should alert providers/staff when needed.

Notification types:
- vitals_monitoring_completed
- stage_2_bp_pattern
- severe_bp_reading
- missed_required_vital_log
- unexpected_bleeding_reported
- patient_added_med_or_supplement
- worsening_symptom_trend

Missed vitals:
If clinician-required vitals are active and patient misses 2 days, notify provider:
“[Patient Name] has missed 2 required blood pressure logs.”

Vitals integration:
Any vitals logged by the patient should integrate with vitals taken in-clinic for trending purposes.

However, patient-logged vitals must be clearly labeled and color-coded as patient-documented.

In vitals trends:
- In-clinic vitals should remain distinct.
- Patient-documented vitals should be color-coded differently.
- Tooltip/label should say “Patient-documented”
- In-clinic vitals should say “Clinic-documented”

Do not overwrite clinic vitals.
Do not mix the source invisibly.
Both should trend together visually, but source should be clear.

Insights/Reports:
Patient side should have:
1. Daily Insight
2. Weekly Summary
3. Trends Over Time

Daily Insight:
Generate warm, encouraging, non-shaming feedback.

Examples:
“Your sleep was lower last night, which may increase cravings and lower energy today. Tonight, focus on a consistent bedtime and a protein-forward breakfast tomorrow.”

“You logged your medication today — great consistency.”

“You reported higher cravings and lower mood. This may relate to poor sleep, cycle timing, or lower protein intake.”

Weekly Summary:
Show:
- Weight change
- Average sleep
- Exercise days
- Medication adherence %
- Mood trend
- Food consistency
- Symptoms
- Suggested focus for next week

Example:
“This week your weight decreased 1.2 lb. Sleep averaged 5.8 hours and cravings were higher on lower-sleep days. Your biggest opportunity next week is improving sleep consistency and protein intake.”

Long-term trends:
Show charts/cards for:
- Weight over time
- Sleep average
- Mood
- Medication adherence
- Exercise frequency
- Symptoms
- Vitals if available
- Cycle-related patterns if applicable

Clinical AI rules:
AI should:
- Be warm, supportive, and educational
- Avoid shame
- Avoid diagnosing
- Avoid telling patient to change prescription medication without provider input
- Encourage contacting clinic for concerning symptoms
- Alert provider/staff when clinically relevant patterns appear
- Help distinguish non-response from non-adherence

Design principles:
- Optional, not required
- Clean and modular
- Mobile-first
- Easy to navigate back to dashboard
- Left-side icon rail inside tool
- No clutter on main dashboard
- Patient-selected tracking stays separate from clinician-directed monitoring
- Provider/staff view stays contained inside the patient profile
- Vitals integrate with clinic vitals for trending but remain source-labeled
- Patient-reported medications/supplements update the patient medication list and require provider visibility
- Menstrual cycle tracking only appears for female patients
- Unexpected bleeding should always be available as a symptom option when clinically relevant
- Build this without disrupting existing workflows
