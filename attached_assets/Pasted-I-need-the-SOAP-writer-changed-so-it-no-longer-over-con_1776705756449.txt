I need the SOAP writer changed so it no longer over-condenses important clinical counseling and shared decision-making.

Right now, the note is readable, but it is dropping medically important content from the transcript — especially patient education, risk/benefit discussion, medication counseling, titration instructions, administration instructions, alternatives discussed, monitoring plans, and confirmation that the patient understood and agreed.

I do NOT want the note to become theatrical, overly verbose, or padded.
I DO want it to be more clinically complete and medico-legally defensible.

Please update the SOAP generation logic with these rules:

1. The writer should treat the transcript as a source for CLINICAL DETAIL EXTRACTION, not just summarization.

2. It must preserve and document the following whenever discussed in the visit:
- risks vs benefits of treatment
- side effects reviewed
- medication mechanism explained
- titration schedule reviewed
- administration/use instructions reviewed
- alternatives discussed
- rationale for choosing one treatment over another
- follow-up and monitoring plan
- return precautions / when to notify clinic
- patient understanding, agreement, and consent

3. These details should not be collapsed into vague phrases like:
- “treatment discussed”
- “options reviewed”
- “patient interested”
If the transcript contains the actual counseling points, preserve them in the note.

4. In the Assessment/Plan, each problem should follow this structure:
- Assessment
- Plan
- Counseling / Education
- Monitoring / Follow-up

5. Under Counseling / Education, include concise but specific documentation such as:
- risk/benefit discussion
- side effects reviewed
- titration reviewed
- administration instructions reviewed
- patient verbalized understanding and agrees with plan

6. The note should remain professional and concise, but completeness should be prioritized over aggressive compression.

7. Do not invent details that were not discussed.
Only preserve and organize what is actually present in the transcript.

8. If counseling was substantial for a medication start (especially hormones, GLP-1s, controlled substances, injections, or chronic disease treatment), the writer should explicitly document that counseling instead of reducing it to one sentence.

9. Shared decision-making should be visible in the final note:
- what was discussed
- why the selected option was chosen
- what the patient preferred
- what the follow-up plan is

10. Add prompt logic so that medication initiation visits automatically look for and preserve:
- contraindication review
- side effect counseling
- administration counseling
- titration plan
- safety precautions
- patient consent/understanding

For example, if a GLP-1 is started, the note should preserve documentation that the patient was educated on risks/benefits, titration schedule, administration, side effects, mood/appetite monitoring, and follow-up, if those were discussed in the transcript.

Goal:
Make the SOAP note more clinically complete and defensible without making it long, dramatic, or repetitive.
