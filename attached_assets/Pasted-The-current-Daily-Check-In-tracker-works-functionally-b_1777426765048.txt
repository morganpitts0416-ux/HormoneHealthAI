The current Daily Check-In tracker works functionally, but the UX does not feel elevated enough for ClinIQ. It currently feels like a long DIY health form. We need to redesign it into a premium, guided, boutique patient experience.

Core design goal:
This should feel like a high-touch clinical companion, not a generic self-tracking app.

The patient should feel:
- guided
- supported
- cared for
- not overwhelmed
- like their provider is using this information to personalize care

This should NOT feel like:
- MyFitnessPal
- a spreadsheet
- a long intake form
- a DIY wellness app
- a generic habit tracker

Main UX change:
Replace the long scrolling form with a guided step-by-step check-in experience.

The check-in should be broken into short “moments” instead of one long page.

Recommended flow:
1. Welcome / Today overview
2. How are you feeling?
3. Body & sleep
4. Fuel & hydration
5. Movement
6. Medications & supplements
7. Vitals, only if active
8. Final reflection
9. Completion screen with insight preview

Each step should have:
- one clear heading
- one short supportive sentence
- minimal inputs
- large tappable buttons
- soft visual spacing
- a progress indicator
- Back and Continue buttons
- Save/Finish only at the end

Do not show every section on one long page.

Example tone:
Instead of “Food & drink”
Use:
“Fuel & hydration”
Subtitle:
“What you eat and drink can affect energy, cravings, sleep, and progress.”

Instead of “Mood/Symptoms”
Use:
“How are you feeling today?”
Subtitle:
“A quick check helps your care team see patterns over time.”

Instead of “Medications & supplements”
Use:
“Today’s medications & supplements”
Subtitle:
“Consistency helps us understand what’s working — and what may need adjusting.”

Replace numeric-only 1–5 buttons with labeled choices.

Protein:
- Skipped
- Light
- Moderate
- Strong
- On point

Water:
- Barely any
- Some
- Moderate
- Good
- Plenty

Fiber/vegetables:
- None
- Light
- Moderate
- Strong
- Lots

Sleep quality:
- Poor
- Restless
- Okay
- Good
- Restorative

Mood:
- Low
- Off
- Okay
- Good
- Great

Energy:
- Drained
- Low
- Okay
- Steady
- Energized

Use numbers internally if needed, but show patient-friendly labels.

Visual redesign:
Use premium card styling:
- warm off-white background
- soft rounded cards
- fewer borders
- more whitespace
- subtle dividers
- larger headings
- calming iconography
- no cramped fields
- no dense stacked sections

The current 1–5 square buttons feel too harsh. Replace with pill buttons, segmented controls, sliders, or soft tappable chips.

Make every interaction feel easy on mobile.

Navigation:
Inside the tool, patients must always have:
- Back to Dashboard
- progress indicator
- clear Continue button
- ability to save and exit

The patient should never feel trapped inside the tracker.

Dashboard card:
Make the dashboard card feel inviting, not task-like.

Example:
“Today’s Check-In”
“Take 60 seconds to help your care team understand your day.”

CTA:
“Start check-in”

If vitals monitoring is active:
Show a separate provider-requested card:
“Provider-requested BP log”
“Your care team is monitoring this for 10 days.”

Do not mix optional wellness check-in with required vitals monitoring visually.

Completion screen:
After the patient finishes, show a warm completion screen.

Example:
“You’re all set for today.”
“Your check-in was saved and will help your care team see the full picture.”

Then show a small “Today’s insight” preview:
“Sleep was lower last night, which may affect cravings and energy today. Focus on hydration, protein, and an earlier bedtime tonight.”

Buttons:
- View insights
- Back to dashboard

Patient-added medications/supplements:
Keep this section clean.
Instead of showing the add-med form expanded all the time, collapse it behind:
“+ Add something not listed”

When clicked, open a simple modal or slide-up sheet:
- Medication or supplement
- Name
- Dose
- Frequency
- Why are you taking it? optional
- Save

After save:
Show:
“Added. We’ll let your care team know so they can review it.”

Do not clutter the main med section with the full add form by default.

Medication cards:
Medication cards should show:
- med/supplement name
- dose/frequency if available
- source badge: “From chart” or “Added by you”
- buttons: Taken / Skipped

Use clean cards with badges, not cramped rows.

Food section:
Standard Mode should stay simple.
Do not ask patients to calculate macros unless they are in Power Mode.

Add expandable helper text:
“What counts as strong protein?”
When tapped:
“Most patients do best with protein at each meal. Strong usually means you included a meaningful protein source at most meals.”

Power Mode:
If patient is in Power Mode, allow detailed tracking, but keep it visually contained behind expandable areas:
- Add macros
- Add meal details
- Add water ounces

Do not overwhelm Standard Mode users.

Vitals Mode:
Vitals Monitoring Mode should feel clinically requested and more structured.

Use language:
“Requested by your care team”
“Due today”
“Monitoring ends in X days”

Vitals entry should be simple:
For BP:
- systolic
- diastolic
- heart rate if selected
- time
- symptoms
- notes

Show a small trend preview after logging:
“Today’s reading was saved.”
“3 of 10 days completed.”

Provider/staff UX:
Provider/staff view should also stay clean and premium.

Inside the patient profile, create a contained panel called:
“Monitoring & Check-Ins”

Do not scatter this information throughout the chart.

The panel should have tabs:
- Overview
- Check-ins
- Vitals
- Meds/Supplements
- Insights
- Flags

Overview should show clean summary cards:
- Current mode
- Last check-in
- Active vitals monitoring
- Adherence %
- Recent flags
- Latest weekly insight

Vitals should integrate patient-documented vitals with clinic-documented vitals for trending, but source must be clearly labeled and color-coded:
- Clinic-documented
- Patient-documented

Do not overwrite clinic vitals.
Do not make patient vitals look identical to clinic vitals.

Provider inbox:
Notifications should be simple and actionable.
Do not flood the inbox.

Examples:
- “[Patient] completed their 10-day BP log”
- “[Patient] logged multiple Stage 2 BP readings”
- “[Patient] added a new supplement”
- “[Patient] reported unexpected bleeding”

Clicking the notification should open the patient profile and open the Monitoring & Check-Ins panel directly.

Premium UX details:
Add small encouragement moments:
- “Nice — that helps us see the pattern.”
- “Logged. Consistency gives your care team better insight.”
- “No perfection needed. Just a quick snapshot.”

Avoid guilt/shame language.
Do not use:
- failed
- noncompliant
- bad
- off track
- poor choices

Use:
- missed
- not logged
- opportunity
- pattern
- support
- next best step

Important:
The goal is not to create another DIY wellness tracker.
The goal is to create a guided clinical companion that helps patients communicate better with their care team and helps providers make better decisions.

Please redesign the current Daily Check-In UI around this elevated guided experience while preserving the existing functionality and database logic.
